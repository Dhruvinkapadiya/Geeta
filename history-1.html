The Bhagavad Gita is a sacred Hindu scripture consisting of 700 verses. It is a dialogue between Prince Arjuna and Lord Krishna, addressing topics like duty, spirituality, and self-realization. The Gita explores the nature of the self, offers different paths to spiritual enlightenment, and emphasizes principles of righteousness. It has influenced philosophy, spirituality, and culture, providing guidance for moral dilemmas and insights into the human condition. The Bhagavad Gita is cherished worldwide for its profound wisdom and timeless relevance.
